# Appendix A — The Bergen Folders

*[The shelf catalogue, as I found it and as I leave it. Descriptions
are mine and deliberately brief; the folders are open to any reader
at the library named in the colophon — except where noted, for
reasons the reader of this book will not need explained. — E.M.]*

**I.** Hy-Brasil. The type specimen. Tracings, the Bristol voyages,
the 1988 Galway transit ("Nothing has a look to it, though").

**II.** Sandy Island; the seen/sounded list; the *Velocity*'s log.

**III.** San Borondón. The 1570 inquiry transcripts; the 1958
photograph, which persuades everyone of what they already believed.

**IV.** Bermeja. The UNAM bathymetry; the rust letter (T., no. 43).

**V.** Grimsholm — the Part I file: the depositions that exist
nowhere else, the Kall chart tracing, the 1817 sounding.

**VI.** Saint Brendan; Jasconius; the domesticated error.

**VII.** *Out.* The card stands in the slot. I have left it there.

**VIII.** Buss Island; the drowned-land charts; the HBC photostat
with the docket. The photostat's original remains "withdrawn."

**IX.** Aurora Islands. Tied with string, marked "practice." I have
not untied it. Some knots are entries.

**X.** Dougherty & Emerald; the controls; Davis's deep cast.

**XI.** Sarah Ann; the guano bond; the clipping with the sentence
the newspaper never printed.

**XII.** Isla del Coro. Thin: one deposition transcript, one page
of fog vigils dated 1981, and sand — a spoonful of black sand, loose
in the folder, which I have had analysed. Basaltic. Warm-water
weathering. Provenance: none on any chart.

**XIII–XX.** The interiors: Kong (the forty tracings, the red slip);
Prester John (the Letter in three recensions and her collation);
El Dorado (the Guatavita sketch, 1977); Mīzān (formerly sealed);
California (the strait tracings); the Sea of the West (the Delisle
transcripts, 1968 — her earliest dated card); Saguenay (the notarial
summaries); Vienna (typescript, cassette, and the society's letter
of non-printing, which is its own small masterpiece of the genre).

**XXI–XXVI.** Waters and poles: Terra Australis (notebook X rides
in this folder — the southern freighters, the brown ink's second
life); the Open Polar Sea (the ice-minimum printouts, annotated);
Frisland (Sorand and Rane); Rupes Nigra (the pole-position plot);
Vardø-9 (the orphan synoptics, complete, four thousand of them, and
the Tromsø working paper); and a folder labelled only *Schedules*,
which contains ferry timetables, tide tables, and eclipse
predictions spanning ninety years, interfiled without comment, in
an order I have not cracked.

**XXVII.** The T. correspondence. Seventy-one letters. Eleven
postmarks. Restricted, at my instruction, until 2031 — the year the
unit's rent runs out, a symmetry I decline to examine.

**XXVIII.** The Anchorage (the Herdis notebook; the bearings leaf).

**XXIX.** The Carrier (Tórshavn; the Pilot's back cover, razored
out and preserved entire, adhesive shadows and all).

**XXX.** The Second Sight (Inishmore; the steadied bearings).

**XXXI.** Grimsholm, again (the 2003 notebook; the cotton bag,
now empty — see Appendix D).

**XXXII.** The Shelf (the Njord notebook; the fouling statistics,
two companies' worth; the chemist's ticket).

**XXXIII.** The Approaches (the Dutch Harbor notebook; the grey
pebble; the barometer, which the estate let me buy, and which
stands on my desk, and which I have never once tapped).

**XXXIV.** Empty. Labelled, in her hand, in the brown ink:
*For the successor's differences.* It is the only folder on the
shelf that is not full, and the only one, I have come to
understand, that was bought new.
