# Appendix D — The Eleven Postmarked Letters

*[Of the seventy-one letters, sixty were carried and remain under
seal until 2031; the eleven that passed through the public post are
printed here entire. The numbering is Voss's own, from her docket.
Voss's letters to T. do not survive, or do not survive with us; the
reader hears one side of a fifty-year conversation, which is, I have
come to think, the normal condition of reading anything. — E.M.]*

---

**No. 1. Reykjavík, March 1974.**

You have been reading the difference column, Professor. Most stop
at the sum. Your inquiry to the companies was correctly addressed
in every sense, and has been forwarded to where inquiries of its
kind are kept. No action is required of you. None ever will be;
you should know this from the beginning; requirement is not the
instrument used. — T.

**No. 7. Bergen, October 1978.**

The two companies that answered you were permitted to answer. The
third was obeying an older procurement, and you should not think
worse of it; a firm that keeps one promise for a century will keep
others. As to your question of method: you ask how to distinguish
a kept absence from an ordinary one. An ordinary absence is
untidy. Look for the dusting. — T.

**No. 11. Kingston, May 1981.**

The conditions you ask for are these: October, the fruit boat and
not the ferry, the rail on the starboard side, patience. Nine
nights is the usual fee for going on purpose. The office is at
four. Hear what is given, hold your course, and do not sing back;
the offices are not antiphonal, whatever the survivors' guild of
1528 believed. — T.

**No. 24. Vienna, April 1988.**

You were not wrong in that room. You were early, which a room
cannot forgive, being itself a wall. Do not write to the society;
the minute-book is more honest than the members and will say so
in its own way. The gentleman at the back who asked after
staffing asks it wherever such papers are read. He does not act
for us. Decline nothing he offers; he offers nothing; he is
looking for the door he closed and cannot find it in other
people's houses, though he will buy the houses all his life. — T.

*[A correction is owed here. In my note from the Bergen train I
counted the Vienna voice among the appearances of the upright hand
— three meetings, I wrote. I withdraw the count. The hands are
two: the one that keeps the registers, and the one that asked
about staffing, which No. 24 identifies, and which I have since
sat across a map room from. I record the error because it is the
only one I have caught myself making from hope. — E.M.]*

**No. 28. Galway, August 1991.**

Dress warmly. The showing is long this cycle. The boatman is not
ours; he is the island's own, and older than his boat by more
than the boat would guess. Go down to the shingle or do not; the
timetable is indifferent; only know that the folklore misleads in
one particular. It is not the island that shows itself once in
seven years, Professor. It is you that are visible to it, once
in seven. Stand accordingly. — T.

**No. 33. Tórshavn, February 1996.**

Your question does the shop an injustice it will survive. No one
is reading your letters between shelves; between shelves there is
no between. You may watch, if you like. Watching is free. Ask
Jespersen to show you the uncollected; he has wanted to show
someone for years, and it is heavy for one keeper, an inventory
of that kind. — T.

**No. 39. Akureyri, April 2003.**

Raufarhöfn, the third week of June, the Marta Rós; say that Ási
sent you, which will be true by the time you say it. Take a stone
from your own ground. You will know the plate when you see it.
One caution, offered from long observation: you will want to
write to me the same evening. Wait a month. The first letters
after a weighing are all the same letter, and we have it on
file. — T.

**No. 43. Veracruz, March 2010.**

They looked properly this time, so it has gone properly. You
asked once what the red colour was. Rust, Iris. Things kept in
water rust. — T.

**No. 55. Milan, June 2013.**

The house you ask after closed in 1851 and did not stop. The
apprentice was not unaccounted; he was promoted, and you have
seen his mark, and you own the children of three of his plates.
When you next use a reading room, sign your own name. It matters
more than you would credit, whose name stands in a register, and
there will come a reader for whom your line in a ledger is worth
more than your collected works. Sign clearly. — T.

**No. 62. Basel, September 2016.**

Yes, the offers will come; they have begun already, elsewhere, in
smaller rooms. He is a collector, and he was nearly one of ours,
and grief keeps the longest ledger of the four. Sell him nothing;
pity him what you can afford; he cannot be robbed and cannot be
paid. He will approach your successor within a month of your
deposit. Let him. She should see the stones. They will teach her
in an afternoon what my letters have spent fifty years failing
to teach you: that declining is also kept. — T.

**No. 71. Dutch Harbor, September 2019.**

You were right about the third place. Bring the blue notebook.
— T.
